  • Kinnow (Mandarin)

Kinnow

  • Origin and Description:

    • Kinnow is a hybrid citrus fruit, a cross between a mandarin orange and a trifoliate orange. It was developed in the 1930s in California and later introduced to and successfully cultivated in Pakistan.

  • Appearance:

    • Kinnow resembles a mandarin or tangerine in appearance. It has a bright orange, smooth, and thin peel that's relatively easy to remove.

  • Taste:

    • The fruit offers a sweet and tangy flavor, often sweeter than other types of oranges.

  • Nutritional Benefits:

    • Kinnows are rich in vitamins and minerals, particularly vitamin C. They also contain fiber and antioxidants, which are beneficial for overall health.
